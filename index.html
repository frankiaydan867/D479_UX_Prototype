Welcome to Taniti!

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Lodging

Taniti has a great variety of lodging cheap and cozy hotels to a world class resort. If that is not for you, checkout the amazing smaller, family-owned hotels and bed and breakfasts. All types of lodging are strictly regulated and regularly inspected by the Tanitian government. Click here for more information!

Shops

We have markets and convenience stores, wonderful restaurants that showcase local cuisine, as well as american and pan-american cuisine!

Beaches

Come and hang out at one of our finest beaches located in Yellow Leaf Bay. Enjoy the beautiful sand, snorkeling, and going on chartered fishing tours in our beautiful little piece of heaven.

Did you know?!

  • Power outlets are 120 volts! Just like in the U.S.
  • No alcohol between 12am and 9am.
  • The drinking age is 18 which is not strictly enforced.
  • Yes, we speak English. Rural areas are a different story though.
  • We have a great hospital and many clinics in the island.
  • Watch your pockets! Not all tourist are here for actual fun...
  • We are super traditional when it comes to holidays. Restaurants will be closed on holidays, so please plan accordingly.
  • We accept U.S. dollar, euros, and yen. Also, many of our banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept VISA and MasterCard.
