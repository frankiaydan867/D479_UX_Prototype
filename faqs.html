FAQs

Restaurants

We have 10 restaurants: five of them serve local fish and rice, three offer American-style meals, and two specialize in Pan-Asian cuisine.

Grocery Stores

Yes. There are two supermarkets, two smaller grocery stores, and a 24-hour convenience store.

Lodging

Options range from a budget hostel to a four-star resort, plus many family-run hotels and bed & breakfasts. All lodging is strictly regulated and regularly inspected.

Entertainment

Enjoy beaches, rainforest hikes, and the volcano. You can also go snorkeling, take fishing charters, zip-line, visit pubs (including a microbrewery), dance at a club, see a movie, take helicopter rides, play at an arcade, visit art galleries, bowl, and—coming soon—play a nine-hole golf course.

Many venues are in Merriton Landing, a rapidly developing area on the north side of Yellow Leaf Bay.

Sightseeing

Explore Taniti City’s native architecture and nearby white-sand beaches around Yellow Leaf Bay. Other favorites include boat or bus tours, rainforest hikes, and visits to the island’s active volcano.

Transportation

Most arrive by air to a small airport that serves small jets and propeller planes. A small cruise ship also docks in Yellow Leaf Bay one night per week.

Not yet. The airport is being expanded so larger jets will be able to land in the future.

Ground Transportation

Public buses run in Taniti City from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily; private buses serve the rest of the island. Taxis and rental cars are available, and several vendors rent bikes and helmets (helmets are required by law). Taniti City is flat, very walkable, and the Merriton Landing area is easy to explore on foot.

General Visitor Information

Outlets are 120 volts, the same as in the United States.

Alcohol cannot be sold or served between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age is 18, though enforcement isn’t strict.

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. In rural areas, especially among older residents, very little English is spoken.

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

Violent crime is very rare, but pickpocketing and other petty crimes have increased as tourism grows. Stay aware of your belongings.

Taniti observes many national holidays, and some attractions and restaurants close on holidays. Plan accordingly.

The U.S. dollar is standard, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks provide currency exchange, and many places accept major credit cards.
